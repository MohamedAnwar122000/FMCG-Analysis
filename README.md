# FMCG Sales and Volume Analysis

## Project Purpose
The purpose of this project is to analyze sales and volume data from the FMCG industry to derive insights. The analysis focuses on:
- Identifying the most profitable products.
- Understanding customer preferences regarding package sizes.
- Determining the optimal size and pack configurations that drive sales and meet client needs.

---

## Dataset
The dataset contains sales and volume data for FMCG products across two years (2015 and 2016). It includes:
- **Product Details**: 
  - Material Number
  - Material Description
  - Brand
  - Size
  - Pack
- **Client Information**:
  - Client Name
  - Client Type (e.g., Supermarkets, Discounters)
- **Financial Metrics**:
  - Gross Sales
  - Discounts
  - Net Sales
  - Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)
  - Distribution Costs
  - Warehousing Costs
  - Full Delivered Margin
  - Volume sold

---

## Approach Used

### 1. Data Preparation
- **Data Structuring**:
  - Prepared the dataset with a clean structure for analysis.

### 2. Analysis
- **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)**:
  - Analyzed key metrics like Net Sales, Volume, and Profitability.
- **Comparative Analysis**:
  - Conducted year-over-year comparisons (2015 vs. 2016).
  - Analyzed profitability, discounts, and COGS across products and clients.
- **Customer Preferences**:
  - Explored size and pack preferences using aggregated Volume and Net Sales data.

### 3. Report Building
- **Dynamic Dashboard**:
  - Built interactive pivot tables with slicers for drill-down analysis.
  - Focused on key performance metrics like Net Sales, Gross Profit %, and Full Delivered Margin.
- **Interactivity**:
  - Added slicers for dimensions like Brand, Client Type, Pack, and Size.

---

## Business Questions Answered
1. **Profitability**:
   - Which products and brands are the most profitable in terms of Net Sales and Gross Profit?
2. **Customer Preferences**:
   - What pack sizes are most preferred by customers across different client types?
   - How do preferences vary by client type?
3. **Optimal Product Configuration**:
   - What is the most optimal size in terms of sales volume and profitability?
4. **Client Insights**:
   - Which clients contribute the most to sales and profitability?
5. **Performance Trends**:
   - How have sales and profitability metrics changed between 2015 and 2016?
   - Are there any seasonal trends affecting sales performance?
